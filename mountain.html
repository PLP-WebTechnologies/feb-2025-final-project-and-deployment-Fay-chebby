A Weekend in the Mountains

May 5, 2025 · Travel nature digital

Last weekend, I decided to take a break from screens and spend some time in the mountains. It was a much-needed escape from the digital world that dominates so much of my daily life.

The Decision to Disconnect

As someone who works in tech, I'm constantly connected – checking emails, scrolling through social media, and generally being available 24/7. While technology enables amazing things, this constant connection can take a toll on mental well-being.

So I packed a small bag, left my laptop at home (though I did bring my phone for emergencies, with strict rules about not checking it), and headed to a small cabin in the mountains about three hours from the city.

What I Did Instead

  • Reading an entire novel in one sitting
  • Taking long hikes with no destination in mind
  • Sketching (poorly, but enjoyably) the landscape
  • Cooking elaborate meals from scratch
  • Stargazing – the night sky away from city lights is incredible

The Mental Reset

By the end of the weekend, I felt like a different person. My mind was calmer, my thoughts clearer, and surprisingly, I felt more creative than I had in months. Ideas that had eluded me for a work project suddenly seemed obvious. Problems that had been causing me stress now had clear solutions.

Coming Back

Re-entering the connected world was jarring. The barrage of notifications, emails, and news updates felt overwhelming after just two days away. But I've tried to bring some of that mountain calm back with me by:

  • Setting specific times to check email rather than being constantly available
  • Taking short "nature breaks" during the workday
  • Keeping my phone in another room while working on focused tasks
  • Planning regular disconnected weekends (already booked the next one!)

Have you ever taken a digital detox? How did it affect you? I'd love to hear about your experiences in the comments.
