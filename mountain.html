A Weekend in the Mountains

May 5, 2025 · Travel nature digital

About a year ago, I decided to start writing every day. I had no specific goals in mind – I just wanted to develop a habit of putting my thoughts on paper (or rather, on screen).

Starting Small

I began with a modest goal: write at least 200 words every morning before starting my workday. Some days, I would write about what was on my mind. Other days, I'd reflect on something I had learned the previous day. And occasionally, I would just free-write without any particular direction.

Unexpected Benefits

As the months passed, I noticed several surprising benefits from this simple habit:

  • Clearer thinking: Writing forces you to structure your thoughts. I found myself thinking more clearly about complex problems.
  • Improved creativity: Regular writing seemed to "exercise" my creative muscles, making it easier to come up with ideas in other areas of my life.
  • Better memory: Writing about experiences helped me remember them more vividly.
  • Less stress: Getting thoughts out of my head and onto paper had a therapeutic effect.

From Private to Public

Eventually, I decided to share some of my writing publicly through this blog. This added another dimension to the practice – knowing that others might read my words made me more thoughtful about what and how I write.

Tips for Starting Your Own Writing Habit

If you're interested in developing a writing habit, here are a few tips that helped me:

  • Start small – even 100 words a day is enough to begin with
  • Write at the same time each day to build the habit
  • Don't worry about quality at first – just focus on showing up daily
  • Use prompts when you're not sure what to write about
  • Don't edit while you write – that can come later

I'd love to hear about your experiences with writing. Do you have a regular writing practice? What benefits have you noticed?
